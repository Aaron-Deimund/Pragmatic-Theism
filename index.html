Pragmatic-Theism

A convenient and sufficiently nerdy place to collect my thoughts, assumptions, and beliefs. I may change the name later.

Some Disclaimers

  • I can be a bit pompas, pretentious or irreverent here. Honestly, if that bothers you this may not be reading that will interest you.
  • My knowledge of theology, philosophy, logic, and science is incomplete at best and outright wrong at worst. Don't believe anything I say.
  • While I will use the Bible and potentially other sacred texts, I don't here feel constrained to conform to what may be appropriate exegesis in other settings. I will not be justifying anything to or arguing with anyone but myself.
  • Some of my current beliefs may contradict one another. My brain right now is a brackish soup of all the ideas and assumptions I've accumulated over the years, some examined and some unexamined. I'm Okay with that.
  • I'm not going to define words that are commonly misunderstood, but only words that are potentially ambiguous. For example, I will not define theory if I talk about a scientific theory, even though it's often mistakenly taken to mean someone's hunch or unjustified guess.

Terms

Miracles
Extremely rare exceptions to the natural order of things, not an acceptable explanation for gaps in understanding.

Evidence
Observable repeatable physical falsifiable support for a claim.

Proof
A mathematical tautology.

In my everyday life I'm fine with the conflation of evidence and proof as in most contexts they are synonymous, but here I will make a concerted effort to keep the two separate.


Faith
The affirmation of something hoped for or a belief in something empirically unfalsifiable.

For me, it's easiest to start with the Bible and adapt from Hebrews 11:1. Even here there are significant differences in translations.


God
The creator and sustainer of our current existence.

I won't make any claims about God's attributes here, but I will avoid using the traditional Christian designations of He and Father unless I am talking specifically about the Trinity.


YHWH
The Judaeo-Christian God of the Bible.

Holy Spirit
An aspect of YWHW assumed when interacting with individuals.

Jesus Christ
An aspect of YHWH assumed when taking human form.

The Father
An aspect of YHWH assumed when receiving prayers from supplicants

Assumptions And Axioms

  • God Exists.
  • Physics works the same now as it always has, or Uniformitarianism.
  • There is a natural explanation for everything, or Naturalism.
  • Miracles are an exceptionally rare departure from the last rule, and are real.

Things That Happened

  • The sudden expansion of time-space some 14 billion years ago.
  • The formation of Earth around 4.5 billion years ago.
  • Single cell life formed around 3.75 billion years ago. I'm open to a natural explanation for this event, but this is currently unexplained.
  • Evolution by natural selection. Humans are no exception and first appeared around 2 million years ago.
  • At some point God made itself known to Israelites as YHWH and established an agreement with Abraham that became known as the Covenant.
  • Through the Holy Spirit

Things That Didn't Happen

  • 6 day creation event.
  • A global flood.
  • The zombie outbreak described in Matthew 27:52-53
